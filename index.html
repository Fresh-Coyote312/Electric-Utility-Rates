The Problem
Residential electric rate data is scattered across hundreds of regulatory filings, tariff PDFs, and utility informational sheets. There is no single source that tracks the actual dollar amounts billed to customers month over month.

Rates change constantly — a utility might update its fuel adjustment charge (FAC) every month, adjust energy efficiency riders annually, and file new base rates every few years. Every one of these changes is buried in a different filing, often in different formats across different regulatory commissions. Riders are never standardized between utilities: one utility calls it "Purchased Electricity Adjustment" while another calls it "Fuel Clause." Time-of-Use structures, seasonal splits, and tiered pricing make direct comparisons across utilities nearly impossible without normalizing the data.

What This Project Does
This database resolves that problem. Every monthly rate value has been extracted from source filings and standardized into a consistent format: service charge, energy charge, distribution charge, surcharges broken down by rider — all in one structured dataset spanning 14+ years. The result is a clean, comparable view of residential electric rates that doesn't exist anywhere else.
